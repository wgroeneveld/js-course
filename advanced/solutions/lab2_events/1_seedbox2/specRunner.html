Lab 1: Gardening in the Seedbox: revisited

It's time to actually do something with the seeds in the box. As a gardener, I am able to select a certain seed by clicking on it, and that particular seed will be:
removed from the seedbox and appear as a plant in the next available free spot on the lawn. You can see the available spots as background image on the lawn itself.
A "spot" should be created as a div in the lawn container marked with the 'spot' css class. By default, it's 'available' and it can get to 'occupied' (both classes).

A plant should be a child of the spot div and have the css class 'plant' on it.
Remember to also give the plant the same class as the randomly generated seed (pumpkin, cherrybomb, peashooter).

So in the lawn you might get: 

<div class="spot occupied">
	<div class="plant pumpkin"></div>
</div>
<div class="spot available"></div>

You can extend your generateSeeds() method to generate the same amount of available spots on the lawn.
